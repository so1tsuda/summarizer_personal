Warner Bros. Discovery sent a clear message to its shareholders on December 17. Trust us, the best buyer of the storied Hollywood company is Netflix, not Paramount. Paramount is led by the billionaire son of Oracle's founder, David Ellison. He put forward a hostile 108 billion bid last week to buy Warner Bros., but the Warner board called that offer inferior and too risky for investors. Why? They're telling Warner investors that the Ellison family, the second richest in America, can't be trusted to back the financing of the transaction, despite the Ellison's offering to backs stop the entire deal with their family trust. That is worth between 200 and $250 billion in Oracle stock. David Ellison rejected the Warner claims. Paramount has essentially accused Warner of playing games and ending the bid abruptly to favor Netflix who bid $83 billion to buy the streaming and studio assets that includes HBO and it excludes the TV assets like CNN. What's next? Paramount is asking Warner investors to tender their shares in favor of their $30 per share offer in cash. They have until January 8th to do so unless Warner extends the tender period. This battle is now one of the biggest corporate fights in Hollywood history and it could reshape major studios and how movies get made and streamed.
